Cathrine of Aragon

About Cathrine of Aragon

Cathrines first years

Cathrine of Aragon was born in December 1485, in Alcalá de Henares, Spain and was the youngest daughter of Ferdinand II and Isabel I, the founders of the Spanish nation and famous because they supported Christopher Columbus travels to what they thought would be to India, but resulted to be a journey to the discory of America. At the age of three she was betrothal to Arthur, Henry VII son and Pince of Wales.

Cathrines marriages

Arhtur and Cathrine married in 1501 but after only one year Arthur dies. Now, Cathrine was bethrod to Henry, and declared to the Pope that her first marriage never was consummated. Henry and Cathrine needed to wait 7 years to get married. During the waiting Cathrine become the Spanish Ambassador in England in 1507 and doing so, she became the first woman to be an Ambassador. 1509 Cathrine marries to Henry VIII and became his first wife. Cathrine of Aragon was 23 years when she married Henry VIII and she was pregnant 6 times while married to Henry VIII. She delivered two sons and a daughter, but the sons died shortly after their birth or were stillborn. Only Mary, their daughter survived. Henry wanted so to have a son who could take over the throne, but since Cathrine could not have more children, Henry decided that he wanted to annulate this marriage too, but the Pope did not made it, partly because Cathrine already had passed through that once, and with that was with his brother, so she could marry him.

Cathrines lasts years

So Henry and Cathrines marrige was annulated, and Henry forced Cathrine out of the palace and also to leave their daughter Mary behind, to never see her again. Cathrine lived isolated the rest of her life until she died in cancer at the age of 50 while staying at Kimbolton Castle. Her death happened in January 1536 and her tomb is situated in Peterborough Cathedral, but before she died, she wrote Henry VIII a letter telling him that she forgave him everything and that she still loved him and that for her, he still was her husband.
